International Cricket Council, abbreviated as ICC, is the global governing body of the sport cricket. It was founded as the Imperial Cricket Conference in 1909 by Australian, English, and South African representatives. In 1965, the body was renamed International Cricket Conference and its current name was adopted in 1987. ICC has its headquarters in Dubai, United Arab Emirates.
